The data journey...

Using a sales data from a department store of 1000 sales over a year, I explored the following questions:

How does customer age and gender influence their purchasing behavior?
Are there discernible patterns in sales across different time periods?
Which product categories hold the highest appeal among customers?
How do customers adapt their shopping habits during seasonal trends?

After loading Pandas and Matplotlib and the data set, I inspected in to understand what cleaning was needed.
I reloaded the data and parsed the 'Date' to datetime type as I knew this would be needed to explore my questions.

Following this I wanted to get an overview of key metrics, using .describe() to do this. It was interesting to
see the average age of shoppers being 41. The min and max Total Spend also gave insight in to a wide range of
customers and their spending habits.

I then began to look into how purchasing behaviours differed by gender. With an expected higher spend on beauty
products by female customers. However, spending in other categories remains similar between the genders, as can be
seen below. However, spending in other categories remains similar between the genders, as can be seen below.

Furthre analysis of gender spending as demonstrated using the boxplots below, indicate very similar spending
habits between genders. There is a slightly higher median spend by female customers, which may help to explain the
differences in beauty spending between the genders.

I conducted analysis of spending over different time periods, including using '.dt.daysofweek' to explore possible
trends. The two visuals below demonstrate sales by month and by week over the year. Despite individual weeks of higher
sales around the Christmas holidays, the month with the largest sales was infact May.

Finally, I analysed spending by age group. As seen in the visual below, interestingly the highest average spend was by
the 18-21 age group. Further questions which can be explored include average spend by age group and product type to better
understand why the youngest demographic spent the most.
